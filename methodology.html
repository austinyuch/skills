The Spec Master Method

Bring DevSecOps, Scrum, and TDD into agentic workflows — without losing the truth

Coding agents produce code, mutate files, and hand work to other agents faster than humans can re-establish context. The bottleneck moves from writing code to coordinating ownership, evidence, and runtime boundaries. The Spec Master Method is an open-source family of 14 composable skills that ports proven engineering discipline — SDD, TDD, DDD, test management, and refactoring — into a governed, inspectable agentic workflow.

Why traditional process needs porting, not abandoning

Agile, CI/CD, and code review still work — but in an agentic team they must get stricter about ownership, evidence freshness, and claim boundaries.

What gets more valuable

Choosing the right owner before work starts, keeping authority surfaces separate from derived summaries, and making evidence fresh enough to trust.

What gets more fragile

A green file that looks authoritative but is not. Mock-heavy tests that look ready. One skill trying to own planning, evidence, and execution at once.

The team-owned answer

Vendors change agents quickly and many decisions stay black-box. An inspectable workflow, evidence trail, and retrospective surface keep process improvement owned by the team — not by a vendor's hidden defaults.

The six-phase flow and who owns each handoff

spec-master routes the request first; spec-driven-development runs the lifecycle; registries and the code-review family verify the evidence.

1

Requirements

EARS-style acceptance criteria; YAGNI Rung 1 filters speculative needs.

spec-master → spec-driven-development
2

Design & contracts

Bounded contexts, data models, impact analysis (DDD).

spec-driven-development
3

Tasks & traceability

Plan closeout for TESTS.md, RTM, SPECS, CR, and issue disposition.

spec-driven-development
4

Implementation

Red → green → refactor with evidence reports; YAGNI Rung 5–6.

tdd-workflow · test-registry-manager
5

Review verdict

review.md owns readiness; left-shift detection flags smells, risks, gaps.

code-review family
6

Optimization

Convert repeated findings into bounded improvements or promoted process changes.

issue-log-manager · spec-registry-manager

Evidence flows one way: ISSUE_LOG → spec artifacts & reports → folder-level TESTS.md → workspace rollup → RTM.md → SPECS.md. Derived summaries never sync back into upstream truth — that is how the method prevents false-green readiness.

The 14 skills, in three layers

All are open-source in this repository and installable as sibling skill folders.

Layer 1 · Govern & route
  • spec-master — front-door router & improvement classifier
  • spec-registry-manager — SPECS.md lifecycle & CR reconciliation
  • issue-log-manager — holding surface for unresolved work
  • local-infra-registry-governance — dev/UAT/E2E runtime allocation
  • shared-governance — git/worktree & ownership guardrails
Layer 2 · Deliver
  • spec-driven-development — the 6-phase delivery backbone
  • tdd-workflow — red/green/refactor discipline (Phase 4)
  • test-registry-manager — TESTS.md catalog, traceability, freshness
  • test-design-generator — concrete test cases from acceptance criteria
Layer 3 · Verify (left-shift)
  • code-review — review orchestration with CLI tools
  • code-refactoring-advisor — detect smells, name the refactoring
  • test-quality-reviewer — evaluate whether tests really protect behavior
  • security-risk-reviewer — OWASP/CWE scan, ranked by risk
  • sonarqube-bridge — SonarQube integration

Scrum, DevSecOps, and Responsible AI alignment

Scrum boundary management

Product Owner routes ownership before backlog hardens; Scrum Master holds routing discipline; Developers execute via the right downstream skill. The method strengthens Scrum boundaries instead of replacing them.

DevSecOps evidence integrity

Test evidence, review verdict, and demo readiness live on separate authority surfaces, so mock-heavy green CI cannot masquerade as production-ready. Security review is left-shifted into Phase 5.

Responsible AI controls

Human oversight via phase gates, accountability via separate owners, transparency via authority-surface routing. Aligns with NIST AI RMF, OECD AI Principles, and ISO/IEC 42001.
