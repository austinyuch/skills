# NAELT Service Framework

**Status:** CONFIRMED from services.json

---

## Overview

NAELT operates six core service categories organized into three functional areas:
- **Legal Support:** Legal consultation, court accompaniment
- **Psychological & Community:** Psychological counseling, support groups
- **Material & Advocacy:** Financial assistance, rights advocacy

---

## Service Catalog

### 1. Legal Consultation Service (法律諮詢服務)

**Category:** Legal Support  
**Status:** Active, Featured

**Summary:**  
Free legal consultation services for crime victims and their families, assisting with understanding litigation procedures, rights protection, and related legal issues.

**Detailed Description:**
Our legal consultation services are provided by a professional team of lawyers, helping crime victims and their families understand:

- Criminal litigation procedures and victim rights
- Civil compensation pathways and methods
- Crime victim compensation fund applications
- Protection order application procedures
- Other related legal issues

Our lawyers have extensive experience handling victim cases and can understand your circumstances with empathy while providing professional and warm legal assistance.

**Eligibility:**
- Crime victim
- Victim's spouse, direct blood relatives, collateral blood relatives within three degrees
- Victim's legal guardian

**Application Process:**
1. **Schedule Consultation** - Via phone, email, or online form
2. **Prepare Documents** - Relevant documents such as police report triplicate, indictment, judgment
3. **Consultation** - One-on-one consultation with lawyer about case situation and legal issues
4. **Follow-up** - Arrange follow-up consultation or referral to other services as needed

**Required Documents:**
- Identification documents
- Case-related documents (if available)

**Service Hours:** Monday-Friday 09:00-17:00 (by appointment)

**Contact:**
- Email: legal@naelt.org.tw
- Phone: (02) 2XXX-XXXX ext. 101

### 2. Psychological Counseling Service (心理諮商服務)

**Category:** Psychological Support  
**Status:** Active, Featured

**Summary:**  
Professional psychological counseling services to help crime victims and their families process traumatic experiences and promote psychological recovery.

**Detailed Description:**
Criminal events often cause severe psychological trauma to victims and their families. Our psychological counseling services are provided by professional clinical psychologists using trauma-informed therapeutic methods to help you:

- Process post-traumatic stress responses
- Alleviate emotional distress such as anxiety and depression
- Rebuild sense of safety and trust
- Develop coping strategies and resilience
- Process grief and loss

We respect each client's pace, providing a safe, supportive environment to accompany you through this difficult period.

**Eligibility:**
- Crime victim
- Victim's family members
- Children or adolescents who witnessed the crime

**Application Process:**
1. **Initial Assessment** - Meet with social worker to understand your needs and situation
2. **Psychologist Matching** - Arrange suitable psychologist based on your needs
3. **Begin Counseling** - Regular sessions with psychologist, approximately 50 minutes each
4. **Regular Evaluation** - Periodically assess counseling progress, adjust service plan

**Service Hours:** Monday-Saturday 09:00-21:00 (by appointment)

**Contact:**
- Email: counseling@naelt.org.tw
- Phone: (02) 2XXX-XXXX ext. 102

**Visual Identity Notes [DERIVED]:**
- Softer, warmer visual approach
- Use accent orange for warmth and accessibility
- Emphasize safety, support, and healing
- Avoid clinical coldness; maintain human warmth

### 3. Financial Assistance Service (經濟援助服務)

**Category:** Financial Aid  
**Status:** Active, Featured

**Summary:**  
Emergency living subsidies, medical expense assistance, funeral expense assistance, and other economic aid to help victim families through difficult times.

**Detailed Description:**
Criminal events can cause severe economic impact on victim families. We provide the following economic assistance:

- **Emergency Living Subsidy:** Assist with maintaining basic living needs
- **Medical Expense Assistance:** Subsidize medical expenses resulting from the criminal event
- **Funeral Expense Assistance:** Assist with funeral-related expenses
- **Children's Education Assistance:** Assist with schooling for victim's children
- **Rental Assistance:** Assist families with relocation needs

We assess each case individually, providing appropriate economic assistance based on circumstances.

**Eligibility:**
- Crime victim or their family members
- Economic status meets assistance criteria
- Not receiving duplicate assistance of same nature

**Application Process:**
1. **Submit Application** - Complete application form and attach required documents
2. **Qualification Review** - Social worker conducts qualification review and needs assessment
3. **Review Decision** - Review committee deliberates and decides assistance amount
4. **Disbursement** - Approved funds disbursed after approval

**Required Documents:**
- Identification documents
- Household registration transcript
- Police report triplicate or related proof
- Income proof or financial hardship proof
- Expense receipts or estimates

**Service Hours:** Monday-Friday 09:00-17:00

**Contact:**
- Email: assistance@naelt.org.tw
- Phone: (02) 2XXX-XXXX ext. 103

**Visual Identity Notes [DERIVED]:**
- Emphasize dignity and respect (not charity)
- Clear, transparent information presentation
- Use reassuring, stable visual elements
- Maintain victim-centered framing

### 4. Court Accompaniment Service (陪同出庭服務)

**Category:** Legal Support  
**Status:** Active

**Summary:**  
Professional accompaniment during court appearances, providing emotional support and assistance understanding litigation procedures.

**Detailed Description:**
Appearing in court is often a significant source of stress for victims. We provide professional court accompaniment services:

- Pre-trial explanation of litigation procedures and precautions
- Accompaniment to court, providing emotional support
- Assistance communicating with lawyers and prosecutors
- Post-trial follow-up and service referrals

Our accompaniment staff are professionally trained and can provide support when you need it most.

**Eligibility:**
- Crime victim
- Family members required to testify in court

**Application Process:**
1. **Schedule Service** - Schedule at least 3 working days before court date
2. **Pre-trial Preparation** - Pre-trial meeting with accompaniment staff
3. **Court Accompaniment** - Accompaniment staff present throughout court appearance
4. **Post-trial Follow-up** - Follow-up and subsequent service arrangement after trial

**Service Hours:** Scheduled according to court times

**Contact:**
- Email: court@naelt.org.tw
- Phone: (02) 2XXX-XXXX ext. 104

**Visual Identity Notes [DERIVED]:**
- Emphasize support and security
- Professional yet approachable presentation
- Clear procedural guidance
- Strength and solidarity imagery

### 5. Victim Support Groups (被害人互助團體)

**Category:** Community  
**Status:** Active

**Summary:**  
Regular support groups for crime victims, providing a safe space for people with similar experiences to support each other and share experiences.

**Detailed Description:**
Victim support groups are safe, confidential spaces where people with similar experiences can:

- Share their experiences and feelings
- Receive understanding and support from others
- Learn coping strategies and recovery experiences
- Build support networks
- Reduce isolation

Groups are facilitated by professional social workers or psychologists to ensure group safety and quality.

**Eligibility:**
- Crime victim
- Victim's family members
- Those assessed as suitable for group participation

**Application Process:**
1. **Register** - Complete registration form indicating participation willingness
2. **Individual Meeting** - Individual meeting with facilitator for suitability assessment
3. **Participate** - Regular group meetings

**Schedule:** 2nd and 4th Saturday of each month, 14:00-16:00

**Contact:**
- Email: group@naelt.org.tw
- Phone: (02) 2XXX-XXXX ext. 105

**Visual Identity Notes [DERIVED]:**
- Emphasize community, connection, and mutual support
- Inclusive, welcoming visual language
