## Overview

NAELT's advocacy work centers on the "Nine Major Legislative Appeals" (九大修法訴求) - a comprehensive reform agenda addressing gaps in Taiwan's victim protection and criminal justice systems.

These appeals represent the organization's core advocacy priorities and inform all policy-related communications.

## The Nine Major Legislative Appeals

### Appeal 1: Strengthen Victim Participation Rights (強化被害人訴訟參與權)

**Status:** Under Review  
**Appeal Number:** 1

**Summary:**  
Grant crime victims and their families more complete rights to participate in criminal litigation procedures, including right to review case files, right to state opinions, right to cross-examine witnesses, ensuring victim voices are fully heard by the judicial system.

**Background:**
Current Criminal Procedure Law provides insufficient protection for victim participation rights. Victims often can only passively await judicial outcomes, unable to actively participate in important decisions affecting their rights.

**Specific Demands:**
- Expand scope of victim file review rights
- Guarantee victim right to state opinions during trial procedures
- Grant victims right to cross-examine witnesses
- Establish victim litigation representative system

**Expected Benefits:**
Strengthening victim participation rights can help victims feel respected by the judicial system, promote psychological recovery, and help courts obtain more complete case information.

**Related Laws:**
- Criminal Procedure Law Article 271
- Criminal Procedure Law Article 271-1

**Supporting Arguments:**
Per UN Declaration of Basic Principles of Justice for Victims of Crime and Abuse of Power, victims have the right to express their views and concerns at appropriate stages of proceedings and to have them considered. As a democracy committed to human rights, Taiwan should implement international human rights standards.

### Appeal 2: Establish Victim Protection Order System (建立被害人保護令制度)

**Status:** Proposed  
**Appeal Number:** 2

**Summary:**  
Establish a dedicated victim protection order system to provide personal safety protection for victims and their families during criminal investigation and trial periods, preventing harassment, threats, or retaliation by perpetrators or their associates.

**Background:**
Many crime victims continue to suffer harassment, threats, and even retaliation from perpetrators or their associates after cases enter judicial procedures. Current law provides insufficient protection, and victims often live in fear.

**Specific Demands:**
- Establish criminal case victim protection order system
- Define protection order issuance requirements and procedures
- Specify legal effects of protection order violations
- Establish emergency protection order mechanism

**Expected Benefits:**
Protection order system can effectively deter secondary harm to victims, allow victims to cooperate with judicial procedures with peace of mind, and prevent more serious crimes.

**Related Laws:**
- Domestic Violence Prevention Act Article 14

**Supporting Arguments:**
The Domestic Violence Prevention Act's protection order system has proven effective and should be expanded to apply to all crime victims. US and UK have comprehensive victim protection order systems that Taiwan should reference.

### Appeal 3: Improve Crime Victim Compensation System (完善犯罪被害人補償制度)

**Status:** Under Review  
**Appeal Number:** 3

**Summary:**  
Expand compensation scope for crime victims, increase compensation amounts, simplify application procedures, and establish advance compensation payment mechanism to ensure timely economic support for victims and their families.

**Background:**
Current Crime Victim Protection Law compensation system has issues including overly narrow compensation scope, low amounts, and cumbersome procedures. Many victims cannot obtain deserved compensation.

**Specific Demands:**
- Expand compensation recipient scope to include more victim types
- Increase compensation amount caps
- Simplify application procedures, shorten review time
- Establish advance compensation payment mechanism
- Add psychological counseling expense compensation

**Expected Benefits:**
Improved compensation system can reduce victims' economic burden, allow victims to focus on physical and mental recovery, and demonstrate state care and responsibility for victims.

**Related Laws:**
- Crime Victim Protection Law Article 4
- Crime Victim Protection Law Article 9

**Supporting Arguments:**
Statistics show current compensation system has low application approval rates and compensation amounts often insufficient for victims' actual needs. Japan's victim compensation system provides more comprehensive protection worth referencing.

### Appeal 4: Strengthen Victim Privacy Protection (強化被害人隱私保護)

**Status:** Proposed  
**Appeal Number:** 4

**Summary:**  
Strengthen protection of crime victims' personal data and privacy, restrict media reporting of victim identity information, and establish victim information confidentiality mechanisms to avoid secondary harm to victims.

**Background:**
Media coverage of criminal cases often discloses victim identity information, causing victims and families to face social stigma and even harassment. Current law provides insufficient victim privacy protection.

**Specific Demands:**
- Restrict media reporting of victim identifiable information
- Establish victim information confidentiality mechanisms
- Increase penalties for privacy protection violations
- Grant victims right to request deletion of online information

**Expected Benefits:**
Protecting victim privacy can reduce secondary harm to victims, allow victims to restart normal lives, and encourage more victims to bravely report crimes.

**Related Laws:**
- Sexual Assault Prevention Act Article 12

**Supporting Arguments:**
Sexual assault cases already have victim privacy protection provisions that should be expanded to other crime types. EU GDPR has strict personal data protection provisions that Taiwan should reference to enhance protection standards.

### Appeal 5: Build Victim Support Service Network (建立被害人支援服務網絡)

**Status:** Proposed  
**Appeal Number:** 5

**Summary:**  
Establish nationwide victim support service network, integrating legal, psychological, social welfare and other resources, providing one-stop services for victims, and training professional victim service personnel.

**Background:**
Current victim service resources are scattered across different agencies. Victims often don't know how to seek help or must travel between multiple offices. Lack of integrated service network.

**Specific Demands:**
- Establish nationwide victim support service centers
- Integrate legal, psychological, social welfare service resources
- Provide one-stop service windows
- Train professional victim service personnel
- Establish victim service quality standards

**Expected Benefits:**
Integrated service network can make it easier for victims to obtain needed assistance, improve service efficiency and quality, and promote cooperation between agencies.

**Related Laws:**
- Crime Victim Protection Law Article 29

**Supporting Arguments:**
UK's Victim Support provides comprehensive one-stop service with good results worth referencing. Integrated services can prevent victims from repeatedly recounting traumatic experiences, reducing secondary harm.

---

### Appeal 6: Promote Restorative Justice System (推動修復式正義制度)

**Status:** Proposed  
**Appeal Number:** 6

**Summary:**  
Establish restorative justice system, providing opportunities for dialogue between victims and perpetrators in appropriate cases, promoting perpetrator recognition of harm caused, and assisting victim psychological healing.

**Background:**
Traditional criminal justice focuses on punishing perpetrators but often neglects victim needs. Restorative justice provides an alternative path for victims to express feelings, obtain answers, and promote psychological recovery.

**Specific Demands:**
- Establish restorative justice legal framework
- Train professional facilitators
- Establish restorative conference procedure norms
- Ensure voluntary participation principle for victims
- Establish restorative justice effectiveness evaluation mechanisms

**Expected Benefits:**
Restorative justice can help victims achieve psychological healing while promoting genuine perpetrator recognition of impact, reducing recidivism.

**Related Laws:**
- Juvenile Incident Handling Act Article 29

**Supporting Arguments:**
New Zealand and Canada restorative justice practices show victim participation in restorative processes has significantly higher satisfaction than traditional judicial procedures. Research shows restorative justice can effectively reduce perpetrator recidivism.

### Appeal 7: Strengthen Victim Economic Security (加強被害人經濟安全保障)

**Status:** Proposed  
**Appeal Number:** 7

**Summary:**  
Establish victim economic security mechanism, including emergency living subsidies, employment assistance, children's education subsidies, etc., ensuring victims and families don't fall into economic hardship due to criminal events.

**Background:**
Criminal events often cause severe economic impact on victims and families, including medical expenses, funeral expenses, lost income, etc. Current subsidy measures insufficient for these needs.

**Specific Demands:**
- Establish emergency living subsidy mechanism
- Provide employment assistance and vocational training
- Establish children's education subsidy system
- Provide housing assistance
- Establish long-term economic support plan

**Expected Benefits:**
Economic security can allow victims and families to maintain basic living, focus on physical and mental recovery, avoiding more problems due to economic pressure.

**Related Laws:**
- Crime Victim Protection Law Article 30

**Supporting Arguments:**
Many victim families fall into difficulty when primary breadwinner dies or is seriously injured, needing more comprehensive economic support. Germany's victim compensation system includes long-term living subsidies worth referencing.

### Appeal 8: Strengthen Victim Psychological Support (強化被害人心理支持服務)

**Status:** Under Review  
**Appeal Number:** 8

**Summary:**  
Establish professional victim psychological support service system, providing trauma-informed psychological counseling and treatment, and training professional personnel to ensure victims receive appropriate psychological assistance.

**Background:**
Crime victims often suffer severe psychological trauma including PTSD, depression, anxiety, etc. Current psychological support service resources insufficient, lacking trauma-informed professional personnel.

**Specific Demands:**
- Establish victim psychological support service centers
- Train trauma-informed psychological professionals
- Provide free or subsidized psychological counseling services
- Establish long-term follow-up care mechanisms
- Develop victim support groups

**Expected Benefits:**
Professional psychological support services can help victims process trauma, promote psychological recovery, preventing long-term mental health problems.

**Related Laws:**
- Crime Victim Protection Law Article 30 Paragraph 1 Item 4

**Supporting Arguments:**
Research shows early psychological support intervention can effectively prevent PTSD development. US victim service agencies generally have professional psychological staff that Taiwan should reference establishing.

---

### Appeal 9: Raise Victim Rights Awareness (提升被害人權益意識與教育)

**Status:** Proposed  
**Appeal Number:** 9

**Summary:**  
Promote victim rights awareness education, help the public understand victim circumstances and needs, reduce prejudice and discrimination against victims, and cultivate social culture respecting victims.

**Background:**
Public understanding of crime victim circumstances insufficient, sometimes even has "blame the victim" bias. This social atmosphere causes additional harm to victims.

**Specific Demands:**
- Include victim rights in school education curricula
- Promote correct media reporting on victim issues
- Hold victim rights awareness activities
- Train victim awareness for professionals across fields
- Establish Victim Rights Day

**Expected Benefits:**
Raising public understanding and respect for victims can reduce secondary harm to victims, create friendlier social environment, and promote victim rights protection policy advancement.

**Related Laws:**
- Crime Victim Protection Law Article 1

**Supporting Arguments:**
UN Declaration emphasizes taking measures to raise public awareness of victim needs. Japan holds annual "Crime Victims Week" effectively raising social attention to victim issues.
