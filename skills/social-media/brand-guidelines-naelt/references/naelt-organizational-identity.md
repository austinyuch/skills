## History & Development [CONFIRMED]

### Founding Story

NAELT was established in 2015 by a group of legal professionals, social workers, and victim family members concerned about crime victim rights. The founding was catalyzed by multiple major criminal cases where victims and their families experienced unfair treatment within the judicial process.

### Development Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| **2015** | Association formally established; began providing legal consultation services |
| **2016** | Established psychological support services department |
| **2017** | Launched "Nine Major Legislative Appeals" advocacy campaign |
| **2018** | Collaborated with Legislative Yuan on Crime Victim Protection Law amendments |
| **2019** | Established victim mutual support groups |
| **2020** | Expanded to online services and remote consultation (pandemic response) |
| **2021** | Expanded service scope; added economic aid programs |
| **2022** | Promoted restorative justice pilot programs |
| **2023** | Established national victim service hotline |
| **2024** | Continued deepening services; expanded social impact |

## Mission [CONFIRMED]

NAELT's mission is to:

1. **Advocate for victim rights** - Fight for the rights and dignity of crime victims and their families
2. **Promote legislation** - Drive comprehensive victim protection legal frameworks
3. **Provide support services** - Offer holistic support services for victims
4. **Raise awareness** - Promote societal understanding and attention to victim issues
5. **Build environment** - Create a victim-friendly social environment

### Mission Interpretation [DERIVED]

The mission reflects a dual focus on **systemic change** (advocacy, legislation) and **direct service** (support, environment building). This positions NAELT as both an advocate for structural reform and a practical service provider.

**Key themes:**
- **Agency:** Victims are active participants, not passive recipients
- **Dignity:** Rights and respect are foundational
- **Holistic:** Addressing legal, psychological, economic, and social dimensions
- **Collective:** Change happens through community effort

## Vision [CONFIRMED]

NAELT envisions establishing:

1. A society where every crime victim receives fair treatment
2. A nation where victim rights are protected by comprehensive legal frameworks
3. A culture where the public understands and respects victims
4. An environment where victims receive complete support to rebuild their lives

### Vision Interpretation [DERIVED]

The vision operates across multiple levels:
- **Individual:** Fair treatment and complete support
- **Systemic:** Legal frameworks and institutional protection
- **Cultural:** Public understanding and respect
- **Environmental:** Holistic conditions for rebuilding

This multi-layered vision acknowledges that victim rights require changes at personal, institutional, and cultural levels.

### Tone of Voice [DERIVED]

NAELT's communications should convey:

- **Dignity:** Never sensationalize or exploit victim experiences
- **Clarity:** Clear, accessible language without condescension
- **Warmth:** Human and empathetic without being unprofessional
- **Conviction:** Clear positions backed by evidence
- **Hope:** Change is possible through collective action

**Tone Guidelines by Context:**

| Context | Tone | Example |
